Why it matters

Photos tell your child
"You belong here."

Child development experts have long understood that when children see photos of themselves displayed in their home — surrounded by their family — it sends a message they carry for life: I matter. I am loved. I belong.

That repeated visual cue builds self-esteem, strengthens their sense of identity, and reinforces that they are part of a stable, loving unit. Research dating back to Ammermann & Fryrear (1975) describes family photos as an identity mirror — a reflection that quietly tells a child who they are and where they come from.

I work closely with every family to ensure that what ends up on your walls — and in your child's heart — is something true, something beautiful, and something worth growing up with.
